Weathering the Storm: How to Prepare for Typhoons

Typhoons and its impacts

The Philippines faces 20 typhoons a year.

When these typhoons make landfall, they bring extremely strong winds that cause destruction and storm surges and huge amounts of rainfall and trigger floods.

These events impact human lives in different ways.

Deadly impacts

Typhoon Odette killed a total of 409 people across the country. Casualties from different Philippine provinces include 111 deaths in Bohol, 96 in Cebu, 74 in Negros Oriental, 45 in Negros Occidental, 28 in Southern Leyte, 22 in Palawan, 18 in Surigao del Norte, 14 in Dinagat Islands and 1 in Leyte. It was supported by an article published in www.observatory.ph wherein it stated that it was estimated that 13 million people were affected, 36 million homes were damaged, at least 1,371 were injured, and 409 people reported dead in the onslaught of Typhoon Odette.

Emergence of Leptospirosis, Flu, and Dengue

Dengue cases have surged in Southern Leyte, Caraga, and Central Visayas. The United Nations Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs reports an increase in infections, likely linked to flooding from Super Typhoon Odette and Tropical Storm Agaton. In Cebu City, dengue cases have risen tenfold in 2022 compared to 2021, highlighting the impact of natural disasters on public health. Humanitarian organizations are actively seeking assistance for affected areas.

Psychological effect

Apart from the physical and economic damage, the long term impacts of the disasters also affect the mental state of victims.

Typhoon Odette, also known as Super Typhoon Rai, struck the Philippines in December 2021. It caused widespread destruction, affecting over 7.8 million people across 11 regions. The typhoon exacerbated the societal and developmental impact of the COVID-19 pandemic. Health services were severely disrupted, with 220 health facilities damaged, leaving communities vulnerable to preventable diseases. The aftermath included loss of life, homelessness, and disruptions to basic services.

Disrupted livelihoods

The impacts of typhoons also affect the long term economic outlook of the victims since their livelihoods may have been partially or completely ruined after a typhoon.

"The concrete floor was all that was left of my house after Odette devastated our town. I rebuilt using salvaged wood. Infastructure Damage

Typhoon Odette severely damaged infrastructure, affecting over 667 structures, including schools, government buildings, and health facilities. The Caraga Region, located in northeastern Mindanao, suffered the most damage, causing over P12.82 billion in infrastructure damage. Other regions also suffered significant damage, including Mindanao, Central Visayas, Northern Mindanao, Eastern Visayas, and Soccsksargen.

What can be done?

1. Prioritize Disaster Preparedness:

Governments and citizens should actively invest in disaster preparedness measures. This includes creating emergency response plans, conducting risk assessments, and ensuring that communities are well-informed about potential hazards. Developing and implementing sustainable solutions and programs to mitigate the consequences of a typhoon disaster.

2. Advanced Weather Monitoring System:

Utilize modern technology to monitor weather patterns and predict typhoons accurately. Up-to-date weather monitoring systems allow for timely warnings and better preparation.

3. Infrastructure Improvement and Reinforcement:

Strengthen building codes and regulations to ensure that structures can withstand typhoon forces. It also includes creating flood barriers and effective drainage systems. A properly designed infrastructure can minimize damage to properties during storms and protect vulnerable communities.

4. Collaborate with Organizations:

Coordinate with private and international organizations to enhance disaster response capabilities. This collaboration can provide additional resources, expertise, and support during emergencies.

5. Community Safety Drills and Training:

Communities and barangays (local neighborhoods) should regularly conduct safety drills, training sessions, and seminars. These activities educate residents on evacuation procedures, first aid, and other essential skills.

6. Allocate Funds for Rescue, Relief and Recovery:

Adequate funding is crucial for effective rescue, relief, and recovery operations. Governments and organizations must allocate resources to respond swiftly to typhoon-related emergencies.

7. Promote Environmental Protection and Sanitation:

Environmental conservation plays a significant role in mitigating typhoon impacts. Protecting natural ecosystems (such as mangroves and wetlands) can act as a buffer against storm surges and flooding.

8. Identify Suitable Evacuation Areas:

Every city or province should identify safe evacuation zones. These areas should be strategically located, easily accessible, and equipped with necessary facilities to accommodate displaced individuals during typhoons.

9. Early Warning Announcements:

Disseminate early warning announcements through various media channels (TV, radio, social media, etc.). Timely alerts allow residents to prepare, evacuate, and take necessary precautions before a typhoon strikes.

Final Insights

Cooperation, mindfulness, and vigilance are critical components in reducing the impact of typhoons. When communities and governments work together to prepare for and respond to typhoons, they may pool resources, knowledge, and support, reducing damage and saving lives. Having the appropriate mindset and staying vigilant amid disasters allows us to greatly reduce dangers. Overall, by encouraging cooperation and cultivating mindfulness and vigilance, we can build resilience and reduce the impact of typhoons on communities.
